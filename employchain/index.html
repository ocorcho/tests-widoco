Abstract

The purpose of this vocabulary is to describe in a semantic manner information related to the EmployChain Ontology, which consists of a set of skills, occupations and qualifications related to each other, and also to job advertisements and people in a search for jobs.

Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents information related to EmployChain Ontology. The data used in it comes from the company EmployChain along with information from the taxonomy European Skills, Competences, qualifications and Occupations (ESCO) Ontology. In this way, this vocabulary describes information about a company, which offers a job advertisement, and a seeker (a person) that wants to apply to that specific offer, taking into account the classes from ESCO (Occupations, Qualifications and Skills required for that job or acquired by that seeker).

Vocabulary for the representation of EmployChain Ontology: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of the vocabulary that represents the data of EmployChain Ontology:

As mentioned before, the scope of this vocabulary is the representation of data from the EmployChain Ontology. To accomplish this purpose, as it can be seen in the figure, several classes and properties have been defined in order to describe these data, such as the Job class, which collects information about the title of the job, its status, its type of working hours, its contact's person name and email, its deadline and duration, its educational and experience level, its employment type, its location and position, its number of vacancies, its occupation field, its remote work type, its responsibility tasks and its type of salary. Furthermore, the Occupation class, among others, has several SKOS concept schemes to represent the NACE code of the occupation as well as its work context. Additionally, the esco:Occupation, esco:Qualification and esco:Skill classes have been used to show the skills, qualifications and occupations that a certain seeker possesses in order to link these characteristics with a suitable job advertisement or vice versa. Finally, multiple vocabularies have been reused: the schema:Person vocabulary, to describe the seeker of the job and the vocabulary of schema:Organization to show how the classes Company and AwardingBody are organizations.
