The main concept in this ontology is the Tourism Entity. There are several subconcepts. First, three key subconcepts are: Tourism Organisation, Tourism Service and Tourism Resource, as shown in figure 1. In this figure we can see that organisations offer tourism services, and that these tourism services use tourism resources. Anoter set of subconcepts are: Event, Public Service, Tourist Destination and Transport Infrastructure

There is a set of properties that are applicable to most of the instances of these concepts:

  • Properties related to their more detailed descriptions (hasDescription, hasAdditionalInformation).
  • Properties related to their category (hasAcknowledgement), which can be of type Rating, Label or Certification.
  • Properties related to contact details and social networks (hasContactPoint, hasBookingChannel, hasSocialMediaProfile, hasMultimedia).
  • Properties related to their location and availability (hasLocation, hasOpeningHours).
  • Properties related to their prices (hasPriceInformation).

All these properties have as a range a specific concept, with all of its related properties.

Next we provide more details for some of these concepts (Tourism Organisation, Tourism Service and Tourism Resource) and their taxonomies, as well as examples in RDF for ficticious organisations, tourism services and tourism resources.

Organisations are classified, as shown in figure 2, in four main categories: Passenger transport companies, Tour guides, Tourist intermediaries and Event organisation companies. Passenger transport companies and tourist intermediaries are further divided into other subconcepts, as shown in figure 2.

Next we show an example in RDF, using the RDF Turtle notation, that describes a Bus operator that offers as an additional service the possibility of taking additional luggage. The company is located in the municipality of Galapagar, in the region of Madrid, and has a certification UNE 170001, several social network profiles, and an online reservation channel. The rest of properties applicable to an organisation would be also filled in similarly, if available.

In this context, we use the class Location to describe the location of any resource, indicating not only the properties related to the postal address, but also its geometry in the GeoSPARQL standard. The conceptual model that refers to this class is shown in figure 3, and next we show an example of its usage, connected to the previous example.

Tourism Services are classified, as shown in figure 4, as Accommodation Establishment, Food Establishment, Destination Experiences, Event Sessions, Holiday Packages, Tours and Tourist Destination Services. Several of these classes are further classified into other subclasses.

Next we show an example in RDF, using again the RDF Turtle notation, to describe an accommodation establishment (according to the model shown in figure 5 for accommodations). More specifically, it is a three-star hotel, with a medium price, with 98 rooms and a maximum capacity of 258 customers. It is located in Mérida and offers several additional services (restaurant and swimming pool), as well as four options for meal plans (accommodation only, bed an breakfast, half board and full board). In this and the following examples, we omit prefixes that have been already used. Other applicable properties would be included in a similar manner, if available.

Tourism Resources are organised in three main groups (as shown in figure 6): Historical and Cultural Resources, Tours and Natural Resources. Each of these classes is further divided into other subclasses.

There are also Tourist or Related Facilities that are divided into Educational Facilities, Event Attendance Facilities, Health and Wellness Facilities, Leisure and Culture Facilities, Party and Entertainment Facilities, Sport Facilities and Local Businesses.

Next we show an example in RDF, using the RDF Turtle notation, that describes a tourism resource, specifically a Congress Centre located in Málaga, with capacity for 2000 persons, managed by an event agency, with several social network profiles, two types of opening hours during 2025 (one for weekdays and another one for weekends), and with additional services such as cloak room, accesses for disabled, event organisations and parking.

Next we include some additional examples that are relevant in this reference conceptual model.

We start with the class Event, whose main properties and relations with other classes and thesauri are shown in figure 7. Events are organised by Event organisation companies, which have a set of applicable properties, such as who is performing in the event, whether it is an open air event or not, the maximum capacity, and the range of dates when it is available. Furthermore, events can have associated Sessions. They can be of different types and offer additional services. This is in addition to properties that we have already indicated for tourism services. Next we show an example of a musical event (a concert) that happens in two consecutive days, and which offers as an additional service the availability of lockers. This event is contained in another event (in a cultural week) and is hosted in the congress centre that was already presented above.
